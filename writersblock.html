When visiting Shinto shrines it is common to pull a fortune by shaking a numbered bamboo stick from a container and finding the fortune with the matching number. These paper fortunes are called Okimuji. They are usually taken after saying a prayer at the shrine.

How do you pray at a Shinto shrine?
  1. (Optional) Donate 5 yen in the offering box
  2. Bow twice
  3. Clap twice
  4. Say your prayer silently
  5. Bow deeply

Okimuji can range from very good to very bad luck. Should you get bad luck, you tie your Okimuji to a pine tree or wire racks at the shrine so the bad luck won't attach to you.
