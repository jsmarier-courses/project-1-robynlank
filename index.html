Whether it was the food, the new people I met, the solo-travelling, or the crazy things like getting stuck on a bullet train overnight, visiting Japan was one of the most memorable experiences I've had. However, some things are truly unique to the country and history that you simply could not find it elsewhere.

What makes Japan truly unique?
  • Paper Cranes: My words alone cannot do justice to the history of origami and folding paper cranes, therefore, follow the link to read about it. The Symbolism of Paper Cranes
  • Deer in Nara: Large amounts of deer freely roam the parks and area in Nara, waiting and swarming for food (which, for 200 yen you can feed them deer-digestable food!) It is incredibly fun and the deer are fairly docile, I had the chance to pet a few!
  • Ichiran Ramen: Offering a solo-dining experience, Ichiran Ramen lets you order through a vending machine that spits out a ticket reflecting your purchase. You sit and dine in a single-person booth facing a bamboo partition which is opened halfway for your ramen to be delievered directly to you. Of all the food I had in Japan, this ramen is what I think about and miss most.
