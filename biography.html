The torii gates at Fushimi Inari Shrine were both beautiful and strenuous to walk through. Thousands of gates took you through forest trails at the base of the mountain where eventually you could climb hundreds of meters to the summit. In order to make it back to the tour bus on time, I had to run up the steps of each series of torii gates, take brief photos, and run back down to meet my group. In between each series of torii gates heading to the summit, there were small shrines with fox sculptures and mini torii gates.

What is the significance of these smaller shrines?
  • The small torii gates are from smaller donations compared to the large torii gates on the trails
  • Foxes are seen as Inari's messengers (the Shinto God of rice) and so fox statues are seen all over the Shrine
